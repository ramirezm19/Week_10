CDMX - Ciudad de Mexico

As we mentioned, on our trip to CDMX, we were tour guides for a group of 10 individuals, ourselves included. We created an 7-day eating/experience itinerary, provided interpretation, and procured transportation. And yes, when needed, some haggling was committed - this was only done when price comparisons were completed, and a fair price for native artisans and vendors was agreed upon. This is a sneak peak at the first three days of our trip.

Meet Team Taco

A group of 10 friends who love everything having to do with food: how to grow, harvest, procure, prepare, and eat it.

Fonda Margarita

Best breakfast in CDMX since the 1940's. Cheap and delicious guisados cooked in clay pots over charcoal. Just make sure to be there early.

First Round of Tacos

After our visit to the Museo de Antropologia, we walked to El Centro Historico and found Al pastor (the vertical charcoal pit) and Suadero tacos.

Tacos de Canasta

When walking the streets of CDMX, you can always find Tacos de Canasta on the back of a bicycle.

Lucha Libre

And to end a day, why not try some Lucha Libre. Is it wrestling? Sort of. Is it acrobatic? Yes. Is it gymnastics? Yes. Is it a sport? Debatable. Entertaining? Absolutely!

Hot Air Balloon Flight over Teotihuacan

Get up extra early, drive to Teotihuacan, and do a sunrise hot air balloon flight over Teotihuacan archeological site and town. And once your flight is done, visit the site, and admire the architecture, art, and historical beauty of the Piramide del Sol and the Piramide de la Luna.

Mercado San Juan

For good, cheap eats, visit Mercado San Juan where you can find great seafood tostadas, gorditas de chicharron prensado, tropical fruit juices, and many more delicious eats.

Casa Azul

No CDMX trip is complete without visiting Frida Kahlo's home and museum in the Coyoacan neighborhood. And if you go on Saturday, you are sure to run into the Artists' market, which has a wide variety of art for sale from local artists.

Tacos de Mixiote

After the Frida Kahlo museum, head over to the Mercado de Coyoacan. We had Tacos de Mixiote, marinated mutton, cooked inside a bag.

Carnitas estilo Michoacan

Inside the mercado, head over to El Guero de Coyoacan. They serve Michoacan-style carnitas tacos. Hands down, in my top two tacos of the trip!

Churros Rellenos

For dessert, find your way to Churreria General de la Republica for some freshly made stuffed churros. There's over 20 fillings to choose from.
